FAQ'S For NEET

NEET UG stands for "National Eligibility cum Entrance Test - Undergraduate." It is an entrance examination conducted in India for students who wish to pursue undergraduate medical (MBBS), dental (BDS), and related courses (such as Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery (BAMS), Bachelor of Homeopathic Medicine and Surgery (BHMS), etc.) in government or private medical colleges in India. NEET UG is administered by the National Testing Agency (NTA) and is considered one of the most important exams for students aspiring to join the medical profession in India.

  • Nationality: Candidates must be Indian Nationals, Non-Resident Indians (NRIs), Overseas Citizen of India (OCI), Persons with Indian Origin (PIO), or Foreign Nationals.
  • Age Limit:
    • General Category: The minimum age limit is 17 years as of 31st December of the year of admission, and the maximum age limit is 25 years (relaxable by 5 years for SC/ST/OBC-NCL categories).
    • There is no age limit for candidates appearing for admission to private colleges/universities/deemed universities.
  • Educational Qualification:
    • Candidates must have passed 10+2 or equivalent examination from a recognized board with Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology, and English as core subjects.
    • Candidates appearing for the qualifying examination (10+2) can also apply provisionally subject to fulfilling the eligibility criteria at the time of admission.
  • Minimum Marks:
    • General category candidates must have scored a minimum of 50% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Biotechnology in aggregate in the qualifying examination.
    • For SC/ST/OBC-NCL candidates, the minimum aggregate marks required are 40%.
    • Physically Handicapped (PH) candidates in general category must secure a minimum of 45% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Biotechnology taken together.
  • Attempts:
    • The number of attempts permitted for NEET UG is limited to a maximum of 3 times for all categories.

  • Ownership and Funding:
    • Government Medical Colleges: These are funded and managed by the government, either at the central (e.g., AIIMS) or state level (e.g., Maulana Azad Medical College in Delhi). They receive financial support from the government, which often results in lower tuition fees compared to private colleges. Government colleges are also more likely to have better infrastructure and facilities due to public funding.
    • Private Medical Colleges: Private medical colleges are privately owned and funded institutions. They rely on tuition fees, donations, and other sources of private funding for their operation and maintenance. Consequently, tuition fees in private colleges are generally higher compared to government colleges.
  • Seat Distribution:
    • Government Medical Colleges: Admission to government medical colleges in India is highly competitive and primarily based on merit, as determined by entrance exams like NEET UG. They offer seats under both All India Quota (15%) and State Quota, with a significant number of seats reserved for local state residents.
    • Private Medical Colleges: Private medical colleges also admit students based on merit in entrance exams like NEET UG. They may offer seats under management quota, which are filled based on higher tuition fees or donations.
  • Quality of Education and Facilities:
    • Government Medical Colleges: Generally, government medical colleges are renowned for their experienced faculty, well-equipped laboratories, libraries, and extensive clinical facilities. They often have a robust academic curriculum and research opportunities due to their association with government hospitals and medical institutions.
    • Private Medical Colleges: The quality of education and facilities in private medical colleges can vary widely. While some private colleges may offer excellent infrastructure and faculty, others may face challenges in maintaining comparable standards to government institutions. It is essential for students to research and assess the reputation and infrastructure of private colleges before applying.
  • Recognition and Accreditation:
    • Both government and private medical colleges must be recognized by the Medical Council of India (MCI) or the National Medical Commission (NMC) to offer medical degrees that are valid for practice in India. Recognition ensures that the curriculum, facilities, and faculty meet the standards set by regulatory bodies. Government medical students are preferred over private because of their vast exposure in government hospitals where they are exposed to a huge number of patients and get better experience. Government medical students are considered top notch because of the high cut off they are cleared in order to get admission.
  • Cost and Affordability:
    • Government Medical Colleges: Government colleges generally have lower tuition fees compared to private colleges, making them more affordable for many students. In addition to lower fees, government colleges may offer scholarships and financial aid to eligible students.
    • Private Medical Colleges: Tuition fees in private medical colleges are typically higher. The cost of education in private colleges can be a significant consideration for students and their families.

The hierarchy based on JEE Main scores typically follows:

  1. Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs): Top-ranked institutions primarily admitting through JEE Advanced.
  2. National Institutes of Technology (NITs): Premier engineering institutions admitting through JEE Main.
  3. Indian Institutes of Information Technology (IIITs) and Other GFTIs: Admitting through JEE Main with their own cutoff ranks.
  4. State Government Engineering Colleges: Admission based on state-specific criteria and JEE Main scores.
  5. Private Engineering Colleges: Many private colleges admit students based on JEE Main scores.

Each institute sets its own cutoff ranks which can vary annually.

The NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) score in India is primarily useful for admission to undergraduate medical (MBBS) and dental (BDS) courses in government and private medical colleges across the country. Here are the main uses of the NEET score:.

  1. Admission to Medical and Dental Colleges: NEET is the mandatory entrance examination for admission to MBBS and BDS courses in all medical and dental colleges approved by the Medical Council of India (MCI) and the Dental Council of India (DCI), respectively.
  2. All India Quota (AIQ) Seats: NEET scores are used for filling 15% of the seats in all government medical and dental colleges in India under the All India Quota. This quota is applicable to students from all states except Jammu & Kashmir.
  3. State Quota Seats: State governments also use NEET scores for filling their respective state quota seats in government medical and dental colleges.
  4. Private Colleges and Deemed Universities: NEET scores are essential for admission to private medical and dental colleges, as well as deemed universities offering MBBS and BDS courses.
  5. Eligibility for Counselling: NEET scores determine eligibility for participating in centralized counselling conducted by the respective states, AIQ counselling conducted by the Medical Counselling Committee (MCC), and counselling conducted by private institutions.
  6. Scholarships and other Benefits: Some scholarships, awards, and other benefits for medical and dental education may also consider NEET scores as a criterion for eligibility.

In essence, the NEET score is crucial for any student aspiring to pursue undergraduate medical or dental education in India, as it serves as the primary criterion for admission to most government and private institutions offering these courses.
